You may agree or not with the vision statements of some of the leading companies you know. But one thing is clear: **they've defined where they're headed**, and their strategies are designed to take them there.

Your strategy will always be stronger when it's rooted in a **clear and compelling vision**.

Think of it this way: your **vision** is your organization's dream for the future. It's where you want to be in 5 or 10 years. Your **strategy**, on the other hand, outlines how you'll get there - the steps, resources, and milestones needed to make that vision real.

## Your Vision Answers Foundational Questions

- What do we want to achieve in the coming years?
- What kind of institution do we aspire to become?
- What will the world look like, and how do we fit into it?

## Why Vision Matters

In my experience, a well-crafted vision delivers clarity and drives action. It does this by offering three key benefits:

### Alignment

When everyone knows where you're headed, they pull in the same direction.

### Decision-Making Clarity

You can ask, "Does this move us toward our vision?" — and cut through noise.

### Resource Optimization

You allocate money, time, and effort only to things that move you forward.

## Making Your Vision Effective

To make your vision truly effective, follow these practices:

- Don't rush into execution before articulating the vision.
- Regularly measure progress against the vision.
- Revisit and revise the vision every 3–5 years as your context changes.

What future are you building, and does your current strategy reflect it?
