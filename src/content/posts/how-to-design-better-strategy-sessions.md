Early in my strategy career, I led a strategy session that wasâ€¦ simply put, a mess.

The agenda was unclear, so the conversation wandered and lost focus. By the end, we had plenty of talk but zero direction.

It was a hard lesson: if you don't give collaboration some structure, you won't get meaningful results.

Whether it's a strategy workshop, planning session, or cross-functional meeting, **preparation is key**. Here's what I've found works:

## Define the Purpose Upfront

Define the purpose of the session before bringing teams together.

## Invite the Right Mix

Invite the right mix of expertise and experience. Don't just invite representatives from every department; ensure diverse perspectives.

## Create a Structured Agenda

Create a structured agenda to keep the session focused and productive. Lay out the main points and allow time for each.

## Facilitate Without Dominating

Facilitate without dominating. Guide the conversation and step in when it starts to wander.

## Summarize and Clarify

Summarize decisions, assign timelines, and clarify responsibilities before closing.

## Follow Up Quickly

Follow up quickly after the session. Provide regular updates and track progress to ensure accountability.

## The Key Insight

Great collaboration doesn't just happen. You have to **design it intentionally**.
Apply these principles, and you'll see your team collaborate with more focus, clarity, and impact.
