Over 80% of companies investing in GenAI see no financial returns.

All that investment, time, and resources with nothing to show for it.

One major reason: too many organizations treat **AI** like a simple tech upgrade.

It's not. **AI is a business transformation.** GenAI is a powerful capability within that broader shift, but without an enterprise-level strategy, it won't move the needle.

## The Problem

Without a clear strategy, companies experiment with AI in scattered ways. They launch pilots, try tools, and burn through budgets, but make little real progress.

I saw one company spend months developing an AI tool to generate automated reports. The technology worked. But they never defined who would use the reports or how. In the end, no one did. The project quietly died. All that effort, wasted.

## A Strong AI Strategy Must Include

### Clear Vision

Define the financial targets AI should achieve and how it will support your business goals. Measure success by business impact, not just technical milestones.

### Tech Capabilities

Specify the data, tools, and systems you'll need. Identify gaps and build capabilities that enable high-value use cases (GenAI included).

### Risk Framework

Outline how you'll address ethics, regulation, security, and model risk.

### Adoption Plan

Map how you'll select, implement, and scale use cases based on business impact, with change management baked in.

## The Key Insight

AI delivers results when it's structured properly.

If your GenAI projects aren't improving your bottom line, the issue isn't the technology — it's the lack of a clear AI strategy that GenAI fits within.
